About Us

We examine the technology-organization interface in the context of rapidly evolving firms and markets. These contexts give us the best opportunity to understand broader the consequences of intelligent technologies for organizational and effectiveness

Ayenda Kemp, PhD

Founder / Research Lead

Ayenda is an organizational researcher. He is interested in understanding how technology facilitates novel modes of organizing collective production in firms and markets.

Ayenda holds a doctorate in Organizational Theory from the University of Texas at Dallas, where he used computer simulations to study how firms leverage social networks to develop new expertise at the individual level and new capabilities at the organizational level. He also holds an M.S. in Statistical Computing, and a B.S. in Mathematics from the University of Central Florida.

Ayenda's current work focuses on discovering and capturing opportunities in markets and firms using new technologies such as blockchain and generative AI. His work appears or is forthcoming in the Academy of Management Review and the Academy of Management Best Paper Proceedings. (Curriculum Vitae).

Sharveree Mane

Research Intern - Generative AI

As a Research Intern for the Gen AI Project at Eola AI, Sharvaree is actively engaged in exploring the intersection of technology and business. With a passion for blending technology with art, Sharvaree's work focuses on understanding how intelligent technologies, such as artificial intelligence and blockchain, impact organizations and markets.

Sharvaree's expertise lies in leveraging emerging technologies to create and capture value for primary stakeholders. With a strong background in AI, ML, and data engineering, she brings a unique perspective to her research. She collaborates with a talented team at Eola AI to examine strategies for deploying AI capabilities across the value chain and organizes knowledge work inside firms to maximize the innovative potential of generative AI tools.
